Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about prosthetics and orthotics

How long does it take to get a prosthetic device?

The timeline varies depending on the type of device and individual needs. Typically, the process takes 4-8 weeks from initial consultation to final fitting, including multiple appointments for measurements, fittings, and adjustments.

Will insurance cover my prosthetic or orthotic device?

Most insurance plans cover prosthetic and orthotic devices when medically necessary. We work with all major insurance providers and will help verify your benefits and handle the authorization process.

How often do prosthetic devices need to be replaced?

Prosthetic devices typically last 3-5 years with proper care and maintenance. However, replacement may be needed sooner due to wear, changes in your residual limb, or lifestyle changes.

Can I participate in sports with a prosthetic device?

Yes! We offer specialized sports prosthetics designed for various activities. Many of our patients participate in running, swimming, cycling, and other sports. We'll work with you to find the right solution for your active lifestyle.

What should I expect during my first appointment?

Your first appointment includes a comprehensive evaluation, discussion of your goals and lifestyle, examination of your residual limb or affected area, and development of a personalized treatment plan. The appointment typically lasts 60-90 minutes.
